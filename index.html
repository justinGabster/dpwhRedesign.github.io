Transparency Seal

Symbolism of the Transparency Seal

A pearl buried inside a tightly-shut shell is practically worthless. Government information is a pearl, meant to be shared with the public in order to maximize its inherent value.

The Transparency Seal, depicted by a pearl shining out of an open shell, is a symbol of a policy shift towards openness in access to government information. On the one hand, it hopes to inspire Filipinos in the civil service to be more open to citizen engagement; on the other, to invite the Filipino citizenry to exercise their right to participate in governance.

About the Atlas

Over the years, the Department of Public Works and Highways has delivered key infrastructure projects that unlock opportunities and support national development. The 2025 DPWH Infrastructure Atlas provides updated data on the condition and characteristics of the country's roads and bridges, covering national, regional, and district levels.

Scope & Coverage

It presents a ten-year overview (2015-2025), with bridge data traced back to 2007. The Atlas is divided into two volumes: Volume I (Roads) and Volume II (Bridges), featuring data through clear matrices and visualized using OpenStreet and National Geographic base maps.

Collaboration & Methodology

Recognizing the value of reliable infrastructure data, the DPWH — through the Statistics Division, Planning Service, and in collaboration with District Engineering Offices and Regional Offices — has strengthened efforts to deliver accurate and accessible information. Enhanced maps, graphs, and tables enable easier analysis, comparison, and understanding of the nation's infrastructure landscape.

Overview

Infrastructure development remains critical to sustaining economic growth, improving social living standards, and removing bottlenecks to international commerce. The administration recognizes PPP as a key approach to invest resources for adequate road infrastructure.

PPP describes a government service or private business venture which is funded, constructed and operated through a partnership between the government and the private sector. The private party provides a public service and assumes substantial financial, technical and operational risks.

Government contributions include capital subsidies, revenue subsidies, tax breaks, and guaranteed annual revenues. The PPP Service handles all PPP projects in the Department, guided by the JICA-assisted Preparatory Survey for PPP Infrastructure Development.

Existing Situation - Metro Manila

Arterial road network of 6 circumferential and 10 radial roads proposed in the late 1960s. All radial roads are completed; circumferential roads remain incomplete.

Three expressways (NLEX, SLEX with Skyway, Manila-Cavite Toll Expressway) function individually and are not yet formed as a network.

Heavy traffic is the norm in Metro Manila. Travel speed on most major roads is less than 20 km/hr, dropping below 10 km/hr on some roads including Aguinaldo Highway.

DPWH ATLAS

Compiled by the Statistics Division - Planning Service

Infrastructure is the foundation of a strong Republic. It facilitates economic activity, enhances mobility, improves living conditions and contributes to the achievement of social justice which are deemed key factors that will help maximize opportunities and realize potentials of the Filipino people. Through the years, the Department of Public Works and Highways (DPWH) has implemented various infrastructure projects under its manifold programs.

This 2025 version of the DPWH Infrastructure Atlas presents updated information on the current characteristics and condition of our country's vital infrastructures, specifically roads and bridges, on a national, regional and per district engineering office basis on a ten-year basis from CY 2015 to CY 2025 and from base year 2007 for bridges. It is comprised of two (2) parts each dedicated to providing detailed information on national roads (Volume I) and bridges (Volume II). Said information are condensed and laid out in matrices and through maps presented in Open Street and National Geographic World base maps for roads and bridges, respectively.

Being aware of the significant application of infrastructure statistics, this Department, thru the Statistics Division, Planning Service with the participation of District Engineering Offices (DEOs) under the supervision of Regional Offices (ROs), has undertaken concerted effort to provide updated information on the current progress of national roads and bridges through the DPWH Atlas.

As one of the DPWH's contributions in promoting the public's right to information which covers matters of public concern, this report presents road and bridge inventory data/information useful to planners, researchers, policy and decision-makers.

Thus, it is our ardent hope and desire that this work, modest as it may seem, will serve its purpose.
